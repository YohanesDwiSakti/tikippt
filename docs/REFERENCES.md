# Design References

Real, well-designed products to study **before** you design a screen, grouped by vertical. The point is not to copy them, it is to absorb _why_ they read as human-made: hierarchy from spacing and weight, restraint with borders and color, one clear focal point, and copy that does not over-explain. Pick the one or two closest to what you are building and keep them open while you work.

This beats inventing a layout from scratch, which is where the generic-AI look comes from. See `docs/FRONTEND.md` for the rules these examples follow. When starting a product, choose the closest references here and record the actual design direction in `docs/UI_UX.md`.

> Domains change as companies rebrand. If a link 404s, search the product name.

## SaaS & dashboards (B2B apps, data-dense UI)

- [Linear](https://linear.app) - the gold standard for restraint: spacing-driven hierarchy, almost no borders, fast and calm.
- [Vercel](https://vercel.com) - clean dashboard and marketing; disciplined type, light/dark done right.
- [Notion](https://notion.so) - high content density without clutter; flat surfaces, generous spacing.
- [Stripe](https://stripe.com) - data-dense product UI that stays legible with very few boxes.
- [Raycast](https://raycast.com) - opinionated, non-generic product design with real personality.

## Fintech & finance

- [Mercury](https://mercury.com) - premium banking UI; restrained palette, trustworthy and quiet.
- [Ramp](https://ramp.com) - dense financial data kept clear through hierarchy, not borders.
- [Wise](https://wise.com) - clean, trustworthy, and a good model for number and currency formatting.
- [Brex](https://brex.com) - bold but controlled fintech brand system.
- [Stripe](https://stripe.com) - the reference for payments flows and money UX.

## Ecommerce & retail

- [Aesop](https://aesop.com) - editorial, typographic, the opposite of a generic store template.
- [Apple](https://apple.com) - product pages built on spacing and a single focal point per section.
- [Allbirds](https://allbirds.com) - calm product storytelling, restrained color.
- [Glossier](https://glossier.com) - brand-forward yet minimal; strong identity, little decoration.
- [Gymshark](https://gymshark.com) - high-energy retail done without visual chaos.

## AI products

- [Anthropic](https://anthropic.com) - calm and editorial; modern without being flashy.
- [OpenAI](https://openai.com) - restrained, content-led, clear hierarchy.
- [Perplexity](https://perplexity.ai) - a clean product UI for an AI-first experience.
- [ElevenLabs](https://elevenlabs.io) - modern AI product design with a coherent system.
- [Runway](https://runwayml.com) - bold creative-AI brand, still structured.

## Developer tools & infrastructure

- [Supabase](https://supabase.com) - our stack; strong docs, dashboard, and marketing system.
- [Resend](https://resend.com) - minimal, beautiful dev-tool site; great use of whitespace.
- [Clerk](https://clerk.com) - auth UI done well (directly relevant to this template's auth).
- [PlanetScale](https://planetscale.com) - technical product made approachable and clean.
- [Railway](https://railway.app) - playful but disciplined infra product design.

## Marketing & landing pages (your public `/` route)

- [Stripe](https://stripe.com) - the benchmark landing page: structure, rhythm, real visuals.
- [Framer](https://framer.com) - motion and layout used with intent, never gratuitous.
- [Superlist](https://superlist.com) - editorial landing with strong typographic hierarchy.
- [Arc](https://arc.net) - distinctive identity without resorting to AI-demo tropes.
- [Pitch](https://pitch.com) - confident, asymmetric layouts with clear focal points.
